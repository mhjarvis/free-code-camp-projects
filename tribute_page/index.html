Fr. Thoms Conway on the bridge of the USS Indianapolis

A Few Facts About Fr. Conway

  • Born April 5th, 1908 in Waterbury, Connecticut.
  • Ordained a priest in 1934 in Buffalo, New York.
  • Enlisted in the US Navy in 1942 and was commissioned as a chplain.
  • Assigned to the USS Indianapolis on August 25th, 1944.
  • On July 29th, 1944, was among a crew of over 1200 sailors when the USS Indianapolis was sunk by a Japanese sub.
  • Spent three days in the ocean tending to wounded, leading men in prayer, and giving the Last Rites to sailors beyond aid.
  • After three days of carrying for others, Fr. Conway succumbed to exhaustion and drowned.
  • Despite his actions, sacrifice to others, and the testomony of his shipmates, the Navy refused to award him the Navy Cross.
  • Father Conway was the last Chaplain to die in World War 2.
